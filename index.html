What is up with ACORN UK?

This website is meant to be a way to talk about ACORN and its issues in a constructive way.

If you don't know what ACORN UK is, this article by Callum Cant is a good explanation https://notesfrombelow.org/article/taking-whats-ours-an-acorn-inquiry. ACORN is a "community union" but mainly does tenants organising, and member defense (direct actions against landlords who are refusing repairs, hiking rents, illegal viction, etc). The membership is at 5000 members (as far as I know).

The only problem: the union is very broken and no one knows how to fix it.

This situation is kept completely hush-hush within official channels. It has been explained to me that "The union is bureaucratic, dominated by full-time professional staff rather than elected volunteers. The union is increasingly controlled top-down, where critical discussion is stifled, low-risk template campaigns are pushed onto branches, critical discussion is stifled and junior staff are overworked and treated poorly", but I only find out about the depths of the issues we face not through meetings but in hushed tones afterwards in the pub. Piecing together whats going on from pub smoking area conversation seems less effective than just writing down everything I've been told, and reaching out to people to correct and fill in additional detail. If you want to add something to the site, email cullen1087@gmail.com or preferably if you know me irl or other social media contact me through there.

Issues

Acorn Oxford, Abersywythe, Lancaster are destroyed

"Destroyed" means different things in each of these cases. Aberyswythe was properly destroyed, like just erased by National who had admin access to branches social media and communication platforms. The reason given to Abersywythe was allegedly budgetary. The Oxford branch was purged, not destroyed, and all active members quit, and the branch became a zombie branch under the direction (for a time, at least) of a single staff member. Acorn Abersywythe statement

Anti-democratic?

National Conference this year has been suspended. Previous motions to change Acorn's structure were not allowed. Paid staff who are meant to be helping branches instead are used to rig elections, e.g. campaigning secretly against motions such as the Palestine motion or the open-border motion. The ACORN No Mighty Oak article has a section where the IWW accuse ACORN of fixing an election and I've heard about similar practices occuring in my local branch.

Exploiting Junior Staffers?

I've heard allegations that junior staff organisers are underpaid and overworked as well as hearing that there is an extremely high-turnover of junior staff, that junior staff are not allowed to unionise, that in one instance staff who irritated national (by being critical of the door-knocking strategy that they were pursuing) had a surveillience app installed on their phone which monitored the rate of their door knocking. Again need to emphasise that this is a second hand account, i.e. important to get someone actually directly effected to verify this stuff (or maybe this has already happened, in which case would like to be pointed to it).

No discussion, self-reflection or even communication between different branches is allowed?

I don't get why the members who left Acorn in my branch left so quietly, same applies for Acorn Oxford. I feel like I'm taking crazy pills talking to members in Acorn today who want to keep all the issues we're facing as an organisation secret. How will most members even know there is a problem if we don't ever acknowledge it?

Bafflingly one of the demands put out by Oxford is to be allowed to talk to different branches. Did this really need to be said? Couldn't Oxford have just done this without needing to ask for permission first?

Financially opaque?

The following claims are from a single person. I don't think they have reason to make it up, but it would be important to substantiate these. The claims are as follows:

  • Acorn UK do not get all their funding from member dues, instead 40% of the funds are from external organisations including the Rosa Luxemberg Institute
  • Acorn National suppressed a motion that had been put forward by the Leeds (?) branch which would have made it necessary to disclose these sources.

Following in the footsteps of Acorn US?

Nick Ballard has described Wade Rathke is a "genius" and in 2022 he was the headline speaker at the national conference. The handbook that we use is adapted from the one that Wade wrote and that America used. This makes me think that we are following in ACORN US's footsteps, which makes understanding what happened to ACORN US important.

Do American comrades know why ACORN US dissolved? Was this caused by inherent weaknesses of the organisation, and if so, are these weaknesses shared by ACORN UK? Also would be good to get perspective on Wade Rathke given he is acting as a guru to our organisation.
