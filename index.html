What is up with ACORN UK?

This website is meant to be a way to talk about ACORN and its issues in a constructive way.

If you don't know what ACORN UK is, this article by Callum Cant is a good explanation https://notesfrombelow.org/article/taking-whats-ours-an-acorn-inquiry. ACORN is a "community union" but mainly does tenants organising, and member defense (direct actions against landlords who are refusing repairs, hiking rents, illegal viction, etc). The membership is at 5000 members (as far as I know).

The only problem: the union is very broken and no one knows how to fix it.

This situation is kept completely hush-hush within official channels. It has been explained to me that "The union is bureaucratic, dominated by full-time professional staff rather than elected volunteers. The union is increasingly controlled top-down, where critical discussion is stifled, low-risk template campaigns are pushed onto branches, critical discussion is stifled and junior staff are overworked and treated poorly", but I only find out about the depths of the issues we face not through meetings but in hushed tones afterwards in the pub. Piecing together whats going on from pub smoking area conversation seems less effective than just writing down everything I've been told, and reaching out to people to correct and fill in additional detail. If you want to add something to the site (e.g. adding a link or document to the Sources section, or issuing a correction to something I have that is wrong), please email cullen1087@gmail.com.

Issues

Acorn Oxford, Abersywythe, Lancaster are destroyed

"Destroyed" differs in different cases and might be caused by different things. The reason given to Abersywythe was allegedly budgetary. The Oxford branch was purged, not destroyed, and all active members quit, and the branch became a zombie branch under the direction (for a time, at least) of a single staff member. Oxford and Abersywythe have demand letters which are very illustrative:

Oxford Demand Letter (I've cut out the signatures from the document).

Acorn Abersywythe statement (same thing is also explained in a)

Anti-democratic?

National Conference this year has been suspended. Previous motions to change Acorn's structure were not allowed. Paid staff who are meant to be helping branches instead are used to rig elections, e.g. campaigning secretly against motions such as the Palestine motion or the Open-Border motion. The ACORN No Mighty Oak article has a section where the IWW accuse ACORN of fixing an election and I've heard about similar practices occuring in my local branch.

Exploiting Junior Staffers?

I've heard that junior staff organisers are underpaid and overworked although like all discussion in the union, it is kept hush-hush, and there is no way to substantiate any allegations. One allegation was that staffers had a survellience app installed on their phone that monitored the rate at which they carried out door knocking. Is this true?

No discussion, self-reflection or even communication between different branches is allowed?

I don't get why the members who left Acorn in my branch left so quietly, same applies for Acorn Oxford. I feel like I'm taking crazy pills talking to members in Acorn today who want to keep all the issues we're facing as an organisation secret. How will most members even know there is a problem if we don't ever acknowledge it?

Bafflingly one of the demands put out by Oxford is to be allowed to talk to different branches. Did this really need to be said? Couldn't Oxford have just done this without needing to ask for permission first?

Financially opaque?

The following claims are from a single person. I trust them, but it would be important to actually substantiate some of these, as I don't know if its precisely correct. The claims are as follows:

  • Acorn UK do not get all their funding from member dues, instead 40% of the funds are from external organisations including the Rosa Luxemberg Institute
  • Acorn National suppressed a motion that had been put forward by the Leeds (?) branch which would have made it necessary to disclose these sources.

Following in the footsteps of Acorn US?

The leadership of ACORN UK think Wade Rathke is a "genius" (as they put it to me) and that collapse of ACORN US was in large part because ACORN US had expelled Wade by that point. Is this account true? Stark contrast to the IWW accounts of Wade Rathke who think he did a terrible job. Would be good if American comrades can provide their account of why ACORN US dissolved itself.
